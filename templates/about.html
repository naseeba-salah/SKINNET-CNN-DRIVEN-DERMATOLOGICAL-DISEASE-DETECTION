🧠

Project Overview

SkinNet classifies 23 dermatological conditions from photographs using a MobileNetV3-Large CNN fine-tuned on the DermNet dataset. It provides two independent AI explainability systems — GradCAM and LIME — and generates downloadable PDF clinical reports.

Unlike black-box systems, SkinNet shows why it made each classification through visual explanations that clinicians and patients can understand and trust.

Why MobileNetV3-Large?

MobileNetV3-Large uses depthwise separable convolutions, squeeze-and-excitation blocks, and the h-swish activation — specifically optimised for deployment on CPUs and mobile hardware without sacrificing accuracy.

ModelParamsGFLOPs
MobileNetV3-Large ✓5.4 M0.22
EfficientNet-B05.3 M0.39
ResNet5025 M4.1

On a CPU, MobileNetV3 is ~18× faster per epoch than ResNet50 while achieving comparable accuracy on DermNet.

GradCAM Explainability

Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping computes the gradient of the predicted class score with respect to the final MobileNetV3 inverted-residual feature maps, producing a spatial heatmap of which skin regions the network attended to.

LcGradCAM = ReLU( Σk αck · Ak )

Single forward + backward pass. Produces pixel-level spatial attention overlaid on the original image in real time.

🧩

LIME Explainability

Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations uses SLIC superpixel segmentation and random masking to discover which image regions drove the prediction. A local Ridge regression is fitted to the CNN's responses on 150 perturbed samples.

ξ(x) = argming∈G L(f, g, πx) + Ω(g)

150 perturbation samples · SLIC 60 superpixels · Ridge(α=1) · Top-10 positive regions highlighted.

PDF Report

After every analysis, SkinNet generates a professional A4 PDF report containing:

  • Diagnosis name, confidence score and severity badge
  • Clinical description, symptoms and treatment notes
  • Top-5 differential prediction table
  • GradCAM + LIME visualisations side by side
  • Confidence probability chart
  • Medical disclaimer

Pre-generated at analysis time — one click to download.
